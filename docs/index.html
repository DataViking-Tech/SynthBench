About SynthBench

What is SynthBench?

SynthBench is an open benchmark that measures how well large language models replicate human survey response distributions. It compares synthetic respondent outputs against real survey data across multiple datasets and demographics.

Methodology

Models generate synthetic survey responses matching real demographic profiles. We measure quality using the Survey Parity Score (SPS), Jensen-Shannon Divergence (JSD), and Kendall's Tau rank correlation. Confidence intervals are computed via bootstrap resampling.

SPS (Survey Parity Score)

Primary metric. 1.0 = perfect match to human distributions. Combines distribution similarity and rank preservation into a single 0-1 score.

JSD (Jensen-Shannon Divergence)

Measures distributional distance between synthetic and human responses. Lower is better. 0 = identical distributions.

Tau (Kendall's Rank Correlation)

Measures rank-order agreement between synthetic and human response distributions. Higher is better. 1.0 = perfect rank agreement.

Datasets

SubPOP — Subset of Pew Opinion Polls. US demographic focus with political and social attitudes.
OpinionsQA — Cross-sectional opinion survey covering economic, social, and political topics.
GlobalQA — Global attitudes survey with cross-cultural demographic representation.
